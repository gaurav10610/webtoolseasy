What is UUID Version 4?

A version 4 UUID is a randomly generated 128-bit identifier. As in other UUIDs, 4 bits are used to indicate version 4, and 2 bits are used to indicate the variant. What is UUID ( Universally unique identifier ) ?

A UUID is a standardized method of generating unique identifiers for resources or objects in a computer system. These identifiers are designed to be unique across time and space, making them ideal for use in a variety of applications, such as database systems, network protocols, and software libraries.

The unique identifier generated by a UUID is typically represented as a string of characters, often in the format of xxxxxxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxxxxxxxxxx, where each x is a hexadecimal digit. This combination of randomness and the use of a standard format makes UUIDs an effective way of ensuring that identifiers are unique even when generated in different systems or at different times.

UUIDs are especially useful in distributed systems, where multiple systems need to coordinate to manage resources, and in applications that need to generate unique identifiers without a centralized coordination mechanism.

In summary, UUID is a powerful tool for resource and object identification within a system, providing a standardized and effective way of generating unique identifiers that are unique across time and space.
